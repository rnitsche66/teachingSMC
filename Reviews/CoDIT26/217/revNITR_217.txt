I know that "C-u 80 C-x f" sets the fill width 80, and M-q
adjusts it. How to make it default? I mean, how to make
width 80 deafault for fill width?

In your .emacs place (setq-default fill-column 80) Or you
can just use M-x customize-variable on fill-column, and it
will set it and save it for you using the standard customize
interface

fill-column is a buffer-local variable, which is why you
need setq-default, not just setq.

Comparative Evaluation of PD, Computed-Torque, and Adaptive
Computed-Torque Control for Trajectory Tracking on a Franka Panda
Manipulator

#_217

Authors: Elshokry et al
Palestine and Spain


Short summary:
+++++++++++++

This paper presents a reproducible ROS 2
Humble–CoppeliaSim benchmark comparing three
control strategies—Proportional–Derivative (PD),
Computed Torque Control (CTC), and Adaptive
Computed Torque Control (ACTC)—for trajectory
tracking on a Franka Panda robotic manipulator
under partial torque actuation (only joints 1–3
are commanded).



However, the paper is based only on simulation. I
recommend that the authors continue their work by
performing these analyses with a real robot.

The paper is well structured and clearly
written. I have added some comments to the authors
to make the paper more readable and clearer.





Key Points
**********

The study executes a two-phase factorial campaign
totaling 120 runs with multiple repetitions,
assessing performance across trajectory types,
speed regimes, parameter mismatches, and sudden
payload changes.

Results show that CTC improves tracking accuracy
and reduces control effort and saturation relative
to PD under nominal conditions.

ACTC demonstrates superior robustness compared to
CTC under parameter mismatches and payload
disturbances, achieving substantially lower
tracking errors and faster recovery times (e.g.,
median recovery times of about 0.7–0.9 seconds
after payload steps, compared to longer or
censored recovery times for CTC).

Torque RMS and saturation ratios are lower for CTC
and ACTC compared to PD, with ACTC slightly higher
during adaptation phases.

The benchmark uses realistic robot parameters and
applies rigorous statistical analyses, including
ANOVA and Kaplan–Meier survival analysis for
recovery times, ensuring reproducibility and
meaningful comparison.

The paper discusses practical recommendations,
limitations due to partial actuation, and plans
for future work involving full 7-DOF torque
control and hardware validation.

Overall, the document provides a thorough,
experimentally validated comparison of these
control methods on a widely used robotic platform,
highlighting the benefits of adaptive computed
torque control in realistic scenarios involving
modeling uncertainties and disturbances.


Remarks:
+++++++

- Mainly a simulation study - clearly structured and valuably compared...

- Phase 1 and Phase 2 are not clearly specified and also a little bit confusing:

   # Phase 1 (PD vs. CTC, nominal; 60 runs)

   # Phase 2 (CTC vs. ACTC, robustness; 60 runs).

and there is also another Phase definition:

# Phase 1: trajectory (2) × controller (2) × speed (3) ⇒ 60 runs.
# Phase 2: scenario (2) × controller (2) × speed (3)⇒ 60 runs.

this is a bit confusing....

Table I: why do we need these 5 runs, as these are pure simulation
studies. Aren't the results of all runs the same, as they are
determistic simulation runs... ?



Some additional explanations of the ANOVA
(analysis of variance) method would be helpful,
since readers need to be familiar with ANOVA to
follow the discussion (for example, to understand
the meaning of F(1,48), p, etc.).


Robustness Analysis. e.g. Fig. 3 A paylaod step downwards would be
more practical relevant than upwards, which would mean to loose a load
during movement.

Guidelines-Section: PD is recomended for slow motions but as no
friction is considered at all in this study this recomendation is not
so meaningfull as friction plays a major role in a slow motion
regimes...



Conclusions-Section: maybe adapt the physical units in the text for better
readablity: e.g. in the conclusion section: 0.0052m = 5.2 mm), 

Maybe it's more worth to do future work in doing those really helpful
analysis for real systems (i.e. experiments) first rather to extend
those simulation studies. And then in a next step you can do some
improvements on the simulation analysis and then extend the more
valuable simulation environment...

Abbreviations to be clarified, at least:
URDF = ??
FCI = ??


The paper is well structured and clearly written, some new aspects
(e.g. singularity free approch, ...) in the area of controlling QUAV are
preseneted


-----

Maybe there should be some more words on the plant model used in the
studies. What about friction modelling? These may play a major role in
slow robotic movements....

But these limitations are mentioned at the end of the paper.  Good and
interesting work but not so relevant for real live issues...
