TITLE:

Parameter Estimation of a Class of Mechanical Systems Using Only
Position Measurement

#_0498

Authors:Jose Guadalupe Romero (Solo Author !!)


Summary
*******
Goal: The paper proposes a method to estimate a
subset of physical parameters of a class of
Euler–Lagrange mechanical systems using only
position measurements (no velocity sensors). The
approach builds a linear regression equation (LRE)
that is independent of velocities by imposing
structural conditions on the inertia matrix and
related model blocks. The LRE is filtered with an
LTI filter H(s), producing regressors that depend
only on measured positions. Parameter estimation
is performed with a recently developed
least-squares–type estimator (LS+D / LSD) that
relaxes persistent excitation (PE) to the weaker
interval excitation (IE) condition. The method is
illustrated in simulation on a 2D SpiderCrane
mechanism (3 DoF, two actuated, one underactuated)
and results show bounded signals and exponential
convergence of parameter errors under the assumed
IE/PE conditions.

Key technical ingredients:
**************************

- A partition of generalized coordinates q =
col(qa, qu) and a partition of the inertia matrix
M(q) so that M depends only on the underactuated
coordinates qu (Assumption 1).

Assumption that certain rows of a sub-block m_au
are gradient vector fields (Assumption 2),
enabling construction of a mapping N(q_u)
satisfying N˙ = m_au(q_u) q˙_u and later forming
an LRE.

Known/unknown partition: some sub-block parameters
and gravity-related terms are known while others
remain constant-but-unknown (Assumption 3).

Application of a second-order LTI filter H(s) to
the filtered dynamics to produce an LRE of the
form y = φ⊤(t) θ with φ depending only on position
via the filter.

Parameter estimation via the LSD
estimator (LS with dynamic regressor extension and
forgetting factor) under an Interval Excitation
(IE) assumption for the regressor.

Strengths
*********

Addresses a practical and relevant problem:
reducing sensor requirements by estimating
parameters from position-only measurements.

Uses a modern estimator (LS+D/LSD) that weakens
classical PE requirements to IE, making parameter
identification potentially more realistic in
practice.

Clear structure of assumptions and derivation of
the velocity-free LRE; the paper demonstrates the
method with a representative underactuated example
(SpiderCrane).

Analytical results that guarantee boundedness and
convergence of estimation errors under stated
assumptions.

Weaknesses and limitations (scientific depth)
*********************************************

- Restrictive assumptions on system structure:
  Assumptions 1–3 limit applicability to
  mechanical systems whose inertia sub-blocks have
  special dependence (M depends only on qu and
  certain rows are gradients). The paper gives
  examples (SpiderCrane, overhead crane,
  pendulum-on-puck), but the generality across
  common robotic systems (e.g., many manipulators)
  is not fully demonstrated.

- Heavy reliance on IE / eventual PE: although IE
  is weaker than PE, it is still an excitation
  assumption. The paper does not analyze how
  likely IE is for typical reference trajectories
  or propose trajectory design strategies to
  enforce IE in practice.

- Lack of experimental validation: only simulation
  results are provided. No hardware experiments or
  noise/uncertainty robustness tests are
  presented.

- Sensitivity and robustness analysis is limited:
  the effect of measurement noise, model mismatch
  (unmodelled dynamics, friction, flexible links),
  filter tuning, and estimator forgetting factor
  on convergence and steady-state bias is not
  thoroughly studied.

- Limited comparison to alternatives: while
  relevant literature is cited (velocity
  observers, power-balance LREs, composite
  adaptation), there is no quantitative comparison
  (simulations or benchmarks) with competing
  methods (e.g., observer-based adaptive schemes,
  power-balance parameterization, composite
  learning).

- Practical implementation details missing or
  brief: guidelines on tuning the LTI filter H(s),
  estimator gains, and conditions to ensure ∆(t) ≥
  ∆min (IE) are not elaborated; computational cost
  considerations are not discussed.

Concrete suggestions for improvement
*************************************

Broaden applicability / clarify scope
-------------------------------------
- Explicitly state classes of systems that satisfy
Assumptions 1–3 and provide more mechanical
examples (with analytic justification) beyond the
SpiderCrane. If possible, relax one or more
structural assumptions or give clear
criteria/algorithms to test whether a given robot
model satisfies them.


Provide trajectory/experiment design for
excitation
-----------------------------------------------
Analyze how typical task trajectories affect the
IE condition and propose trajectory design or
probing signals that guarantee IE with minimal
disruption to the task. Include theoretical and
numerical examples showing how IE can be enforced
or detected online.


Robustness analysis
-------------------

Study estimator sensitivity to measurement noise
and model mismatch (unmodelled friction, parameter
drift). Provide bounds on parameter estimation
error under bounded noise, and include simulations
with realistic sensor noise and disturbances.

Analyze effect of filter H(s) and estimator
parameters (forgetting factor, gains) on transient
and steady-state performance; provide tuning
recommendations.

Compare with alternative approaches
-----------------------------------

Include quantitative comparisons (simulations and,
if possible, experiments) with at least one or two
established methods: (a) velocity-observer–based
adaptive estimation, (b) power-balance LRE
approaches, and (c) classical LS with PE (where
applicable). Compare convergence speed, accuracy,
and robustness.

Add experimental validation
---------------------------
Implement the estimator on hardware (e.g., a small
crane testbed or robotic manipulator) to validate
performance in the presence of measurement noise,
unmodelled dynamics, sensor delays, and actuator
limitations. Report practical issues encountered
and how they were mitigated.


Provide implementation guidance
-------------------------------
Add a dedicated section with step-by-step
implementation guidance: how to choose filter pole
λ, estimator gains (α, β, γ, p, F), initial
conditions, numerical considerations (avoid
singularities when computing
adjugates/determinants), and computational
complexity.

Include pseudo-code or algorithm box for the full
estimation loop (filtering, LSD updates, parameter
extraction).


Theoretical extensions
----------------------
Investigate convergence to true parameters under
weaker excitation (e.g., finite-time or
interval-wise conditions) or propose
composite/adaptive extensions (e.g., concurrent
learning, composite learning) to improve
convergence without stronger excitation.

Explore combining the estimator with adaptive
control to handle simultaneous trajectory tracking
and parameter identification, and provide
stability proofs for the closed-loop adaptation.

Improve clarity and reproducibility
-----------------------------------

Provide clearer derivations for the critical steps
transforming dynamics into the LRE, with
intermediate steps and justification for using the
filter H(s).

Share simulation parameters and code (or detailed
numerical recipes) in an appendix to allow
reproduction of results (note: provide these
within the paper or as supplementary material, not
as external links unless requested).


Address numerical and algebraic issues
--------------------------------------

Discuss numerical stability when computing ∆,
adj{·}, and determinant-based quantities used by
the LSD algorithm. Suggest regularization
strategies or alternatives when matrices become
ill-conditioned.


Discuss limitations explicitly in conclusion
---------------------------------------------
Add an explicit, concise limitations paragraph
describing scope, practical constraints, and
scenarios where the method is not appropriate;
suggest future research directions accordingly.

Short prioritized action list (if limited time)
+++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++++

  baseline method.

- Provide implementation pseudo-code and concrete
  tuning examples for H(s) and LSD gains.

- Add discussion and examples on how to ensure or
  verify IE in practice (trajectory design or
  online monitoring).
