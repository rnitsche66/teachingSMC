TITLE:

Finite-Time Nonsingular Terminal Sliding Mode Control for 3D
Trajectory Tracking of a Quadrotor UAV under Exogenous Perturbations

#_0637

Authors: Edin Avdukic, Almir Salihbegovic, Emir Sokic and
         Nedim Osmic Sarajevo


**************************************************
Short summary:
**************

Summary (3–6 sentences)

The paper proposes a nonsingular terminal sliding
mode control (NTSMC) scheme for 3D trajectory
tracking of a quadrotor UAV using the full
nonlinear six-degree-of-freedom model.

A Lyapunov-based analysis is provided to prove
finite-time convergence of sliding variables and
tracking errors under bounded disturbances and
modeling uncertainty.

A high-gain adaptation (HGA) mechanism is
incorporated to reduce chattering; three
simulation scenarios compare NTSMC (with and
without HGA) to first-order SMC (FOSMC) and
second-order SMC (SOSMC) under persistent
disturbances and aggressive maneuvers.

Results show the NTSMC gives a favorable trade-off
between robustness and transient behavior (more
consistent closed-loop performance), while HGA
reduces control switching at the expense of some
tracking accuracy.

Suggestions for improvement (specific, actionable)
**************************************************

Structure and clarity

- Split Section II and III into clearly labeled
  subsections (model, control design, stability
  proof, HGA modification) and add a short roadmap
  at the start of each section so readers can
  follow the flow from model assumptions to
  controller synthesis. Include a compact notation
  table listing all symbols and nominal parameter
  values.

Theoretical exposition

- State all assumptions explicitly (disturbance
  bounds, required measurements, initial condition
  restrictions, and actuator limits). After each
  Lya­punov/finite-time claim, summarize in plain
  language what the LMI/gain conditions mean
  physically and how conservative they are.

Experiments / simulations and evaluation

- Broaden evaluation scenarios: add more
  realistic/representative disturbances (e.g.,
  wind gust profiles, random road-like inputs for
  position tasks), different reference
  trajectories, and Monte Carlo runs with
  parameter perturbations and sensor noise. Report
  quantitative metrics (RMSE, max error, settling
  time) with basic statistics (mean ± std) in a
  table.

Baselines and comparisons

- Include at least one additional baseline (e.g.,
  EKF-based or model-predictive controller, or a
  tuned linear controller) and compare both
  tracking performance and control
  effort/computation time to contextualize
  advantages and trade-offs.

Discussion and limitations

- Expand the discussion on practical implications:
  explain why HGA produces transient accuracy loss
  and how to balance chattering reduction vs
  robustness; discuss required sampling rate,
  actuator bandwidth, and how parameter
  uncertainty or unmodeled dynamics affect
  guarantees. Add a short subsection on
  limitations and possible mitigation (adaptive
  gains, observer augmentation).

References and reproducibility

- Add recent, directly relevant works on SMC
  variants, HGA methods, and experimental
  quadrotor studies to strengthen related-work
  context. Improve reproducibility by providing a
  table of all simulation parameters, controller
  gains, solver settings (time step, solver), and
  — if possible — pseudocode or code snippets for
  the controller/HGA so reviewers can reproduce
  key results.
